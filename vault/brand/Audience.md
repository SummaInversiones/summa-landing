# Audience

## Primary segment
**Argentine savers, 25–45 years old.**

Key traits inferable from the landing copy:
- Have the **habit of saving** but lack investing literacy.
- Probably hold ARS (and likely some USD) outside the formal financial system or in low-yield instruments.
- Frame finance as **distant / hostile** — "siempre tuvimos que mirar de lejos al mundo financiero".
- Read Spanish (rioplatense), use voseo natively. Untranslated English would feel cold.
- Are mobile-first (the conversion target is **App Store / Google Play download**, not signup).

## What they need from the page
1. **Reassurance that the product is regulated** — CNV is explicit, Alfy is named.
2. **Concrete proof it works** — the 4-step Process shows real app screenshots.
3. **A way to feel the value before downloading** — the calculator ("Vos solo vs con Palm").
4. **No condescension** — the voice is friendly, but disciplined, not infantilizing.

## What they don't want
- Heavy financial jargon up front.
- "AI" framing as a sales hook (removed deliberately — see [[Rebrand from Summa]]).
- Pressure-tactics ("Act now!", "Limited spots!").
- Inflated social-proof counters (Summa had a hardcoded "+847 personas" — see [[../history/Decisions]]).
