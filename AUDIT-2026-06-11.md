# Palm landing — audit 2026-06-11

Scope: quality, polish, SEO, GEO (AI-search), animations, accessibility, taste.
Method: full read of `app/`, `components/`, `lib/`, `public/`, vault context
(`Decisions.md`, `Anti-patterns.md`, `Motion System.md`, `Voice.md`, `SEO.md`),
test suite run (10/10 passing). Respects locked decisions — nothing below
proposes reverting something the vault already validated.

**Overall: strong foundation.** Metadata, robots + AI crawlers, sitemap,
FAQPage/FinancialService JSON-LD, `llms.txt`, reduce-motion contracts and the
card motion system are all above the bar for a pre-launch landing. The issues
that matter are trust gaps (dead legal links, guessed social URLs), one factual
drift that AI assistants will quote verbatim, and asset weight.

## 1. Critical — fix before (or at) launch

### 1.1 Dead legal links on a CNV-regulated fintech
`components/Footer.tsx:24-26` ("Términos de uso", "Privacidad" → `href="#"`) and
`Footer.tsx:115-117` ("Términos", "Privacidad", "Cookies" → `href="#"`).

Five dead anchors in the footer of a site whose whole pitch is "sin letra
chica". This is the single biggest credibility issue: users who click get
nothing, Google's E-E-A-T signals for YMYL (finance) pages weigh legal/trust
pages heavily, and AI assistants checking trustworthiness look for them too.

**Fix:** create `/terminos` and `/privacidad` (even minimal, honest versions:
who operates Palm, data handling, jurisdiction), or remove the links until the
pages exist. A `#` link is worse than no link. Drop "Cookies" if there's no
cookie banner/policy (the site appears to set none — that's a clean story:
say so in the privacy page).

### 1.2 FAQ + llms.txt tell AI assistants the app is downloadable — it isn't
`lib/faq.ts:36-38`: "Bajate la app desde la App Store o Google Play, conectá tu
resumen bancario…" — but the hero buttons say **"Próximamente"** and link to the
waitlist (`components/Hero.tsx:17-24`). `public/llms.txt` "Cómo funciona" has
the same drift.

This is the exact content ChatGPT/Claude/Perplexity will quote when someone
asks "cómo empiezo a usar Palm". They'll send users to app stores where the app
doesn't exist — a worse outcome than no answer.

**Fix:** reword that FAQ answer to the waitlist reality, e.g. "Anotate en la
lista de espera en palminversiones.com — te avisamos apenas abramos. Cuando la
app esté disponible, conectás tu resumen bancario y…". Update `llms.txt` to
match. (Editing `lib/faq.ts` updates page + JSON-LD together — that part of the
design is working as intended.) When the app ships, revert to the current copy.

### 1.3 Guessed social URLs may 404
`components/Footer.tsx:30` — the code itself notes X and LinkedIn URLs are
"best-guesses from the brand handle". A footer social link that 404s is a trust
leak, and `sameAs` consistency matters for the Organization knowledge graph.

**Fix:** verify both; remove any that aren't live yet. Add the verified ones to
`StructuredData.tsx` `sameAs` (currently Instagram only).

## 2. High value — SEO / GEO

### 2.1 Image weight + formats (also the LCP)
Everything in `public/` is PNG. Notables:

- `public/mockups/Hero-section.png` — 176 KB, **the LCP element**, served as
  plain `<img>` with no `width`/`height` attributes (`Hero.tsx:46`) → layout
  shift while it loads, no responsive variants. `fetchPriority="high"` ✅.
- `public/mockups/palm-logo.png` — 184 KB for a ~32px navbar logo. It's also a
  byte-duplicate of `Palm logo gradient.png`.
- `public/PalmLogoGradient.png` (142 KB), `LinkedIn cover - 2.png` (816 KB),
  the `Screenshot 2026-05-27 *.png` files (duplicates of the `screen-*.png`
  set), `Card 1/`, `Card 2/` — all deployed to the Worker, none referenced by
  the app.

**Fix, in order of impact:**
1. Add explicit `width`/`height` (intrinsic ratio) to the hero img — kills CLS.
2. Convert hero + logos to WebP (or AVIF) — expect ~60-70% smaller; export the
   logo at the sizes it's actually rendered (or as SVG if the source exists).
3. Delete unused assets from `public/` (they ship with every deploy).
4. Optional: wire `next/image` through the OpenNext Cloudflare image loader for
   automatic srcset — fine to defer; static optimized files get 90% of it.

### 2.2 Add Offers to the FinancialService schema
`components/StructuredData.tsx` — the two plans and the `$14.999 ARS/mes` price
are public on the page (`Pillars.tsx`), so they're grounded claims, but the
schema doesn't carry them. Pricing is one of the highest-signal facts for both
rich results and AI answers ("¿cuánto cuesta Palm?").

**Fix:** add an `offers` array (two `Offer`s: free plan, paid plan with
`priceSpecification` `ARS 14999`/month, `availability: PreOrder` while it's
invitation-only). Keep it in lockstep with `Pillars.tsx` — same rule as the
calculator disclaimer.

### 2.3 Layout-level canonical leaks to child pages
`app/layout.tsx:43` sets `alternates: { canonical: "/" }` in the **root
layout**, so any page that doesn't override it (`/animaciones`,
`/cards-nuevas`) inherits a canonical pointing at the homepage while also being
`noindex` — contradictory signals. Harmless today, a footgun for every future
page that forgets to override.

**Fix:** move `alternates` out of the layout into the home page's metadata
(`app/page.tsx` currently exports none — add it there).

### 2.4 Sitemap `lastModified: new Date()`
`app/sitemap.ts:9,15` — every deploy stamps "modified now" even when nothing
changed, which teaches crawlers the field is noise. Use real last-change dates
(hardcoded constants are fine at two URLs).

### 2.5 Search Console — still pending
Already on the vault's "not done" list; repeating because it's free and gates
everything else: submit the domain + sitemap, confirm indexing of both URLs.

### 2.6 GEO content gap (when you're ready to write)
The site can only be cited for facts it states. Two cheap, high-leverage adds
that fit the existing structure (both as FAQ entries in `lib/faq.ts`, no new
pages needed): "¿Palm es seguro? ¿Qué pasa con mi plata si Palm cierra?"
(custody answer — the broker holds assets, CNV framing) and "¿Palm vs hacerlo
solo en un broker?" (the comparison AI assistants actually get asked). Both are
grounded in claims already on the page.

## 3. Accessibility & UX polish

### 3.1 `:focus-visible` coverage is partial
Have it: `.menu-link`, calc range/select, `.shiny-cta`. Missing it: `.store-btn`
(hero), `.nav-cta` + `.nav-burger` (navbar), `.fnl__input`/`.fnl__btn` (the
waitlist form — input has a `:focus` border-color change only), `.footer-col__link`,
`.pillar-cta`. Keyboard users tab through the page's primary conversion path
(nav CTA → store buttons → waitlist) mostly blind.

**Fix:** one shared rule, consistent with the existing treatment:
`a:focus-visible, button:focus-visible { outline: 2px solid var(--gold); outline-offset: 3px; }`
scoped to the interactive classes above (or globally with exceptions).

### 3.2 Smooth scroll ignores reduced motion
`app/sections.css:36` — `html { scroll-behavior: smooth }` applies even under
`prefers-reduced-motion: reduce`. Anchor jumps (navbar, burger, footer) animate
for users who asked for no motion. Everything else honors the contract; this is
the one gap.

**Fix:** wrap in `@media (prefers-reduced-motion: no-preference)`.

### 3.3 Headings are invisible without JS
Every section `h2` carries `data-split-words`, and `app/sections.css:2438`
holds them at `visibility: hidden` until JS adds `.split-ready`. If JS fails or
is blocked, the page renders with no section headings. Crawlers that execute JS
(Googlebot) are fine; HTML-only fetchers (several AI crawlers you explicitly
allow in `robots.ts`) still see the text in source, so SEO impact is low — this
is a progressive-enhancement robustness gap.

**Fix (cheap):** add `<noscript><style>[data-split-words]{visibility:visible}</style></noscript>`
in the layout head.

### 3.4 Small a11y nits
- `Hero.tsx:46` — alt `"Palm·invest en la app móvil"`: "Palm·invest" reads like
  a typo'd brand. Suggest `"La app de Palm mostrando tu portafolio"`.
- `Calculator.tsx:68,75` — bar values render `"12 a"`. The abbreviation is
  cryptic for everyone and worse aloud ("doce a"). Spell `años` (space allows)
  or add an `aria-label` with the full text.
- `Marquee` is `aria-hidden` — fine since the claims repeat in the footer, but
  note "Broker partner: Alfy Inversiones" is otherwise only in the FAQ; if Alfy
  matters for trust, consider it somewhere AT-visible on home.

## 4. Animations

The system is in genuinely good shape: per-card async phase loops with explicit
cancellation, `useReducedMotion` contracts, IO-paused shader, the raster rule,
selector-driven enhancements that can't break when markup changes. Findings are
minor:

- **3.2 above is the only real gap** (smooth scroll vs reduced motion).
- `ClientEnhancements.tsx:163-198` — the `[data-count]` branch now matches
  nothing on any page (the bento tiles that used it are gone). Same "harmless
  empty NodeList" policy as CardAnimations' `.pcard` branch, so fine to keep —
  just noting it's two dead branches now; if a third accumulates, prune.
- Tilt (`data-tilt`) writes `transform` directly while PCard entrance also
  animates transform — they never overlap (pillars aren't PCards), no conflict.
  No action; documenting so nobody "unifies" them.
- Taste check on the new card rows: entrance stagger `index * 0.1s` across a
  4-up row reads well; idle blob drift only on navy cards matches the vault
  rule. No changes suggested.

## 5. Taste / copy

Voice is impressively consistent (voseo, "plata", no exclamation marks, kw
italics discipline). Three slips:

1. **"Apartamento"** — `components/CalcGallery/useCalc.ts:15`. Rioplatense says
   **"Departamento"** (or "Depto"). For a brand whose voice doc opens with
   "El amigo que entiende de plata", this is the kind of word a porteño
   notices instantly. ("Cuota inicial" on line 14 is defensible, though
   "Anticipo" is the more common AR term for property down payments.)
2. **`mailto:dev@palminversiones.com`** as the public contact
   (`Footer.tsx:23`) — a `dev@` address in the "Compañía → Contacto" slot reads
   like plumbing. `hola@` matches the voice; keep dev@ for internal.
3. **Footer legal line** `© 2026 Palm Inversiones · Regulado por CNV · Hecho en
   Argentina 🇦🇷` (`Footer.tsx:112`) — two middle dots in a static line, which
   is the exact pattern the vault's own anti-pattern list caps at one per line
   (the trust strip was rewritten to comma + period for this reason). Suggest
   the same treatment: `© 2026 Palm Inversiones. Regulado por CNV, hecho en
   Argentina 🇦🇷`. (Flag, not a demand — if this one was consciously accepted,
   add it to Decisions.md so the next audit doesn't re-flag it.)
